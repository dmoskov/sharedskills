# Anatomical Leg References for 3D Modeling

## Overview
This document compiles essential anatomical references for accurate 3D modeling of human legs, with focus on bone structure, muscle groups, joint mechanics, and motion dynamics.

## 1. Skeletal Structure

### Hip Joint (Acetabulofemoral Joint)
- **Type**: Ball-and-socket synovial joint
- **Range of Motion**:
  - Flexion: 0-125°
  - Extension: 0-30°
  - Abduction: 0-45°
  - Adduction: 0-30°
  - Internal rotation: 0-45°
  - External rotation: 0-45°

### Femur (Thighbone)
- **Length**: ~26.7% of body height
- **Key Features**:
  - Head: 45-56mm diameter
  - Neck: 125° angle (coxa valga/vara variations)
  - Greater trochanter: Lateral prominence
  - Lesser trochanter: Medial prominence
  - Linea aspera: Posterior ridge for muscle attachment

### Knee Joint
- **Components**:
  - Tibiofemoral joint (main hinge)
  - Patellofemoral joint (kneecap tracking)
- **Range of Motion**:
  - Flexion: 0-140°
  - Extension: 0° (up to 10° hyperextension)
  - Internal rotation: 0-10° (when flexed)
  - External rotation: 0-30° (when flexed)

### Patella (Kneecap)
- **Dimensions**: 
  - Width: 40-45mm
  - Height: 45-50mm
  - Thickness: 20-25mm
- **Function**: Increases quadriceps leverage by 30%

### Tibia (Shinbone)
- **Length**: ~22% of body height
- **Key Features**:
  - Tibial plateau: Articulates with femur
  - Tibial tuberosity: Patellar tendon attachment
  - Medial malleolus: Inner ankle bone

### Fibula
- **Length**: Slightly shorter than tibia
- **Function**: 
  - Bears 10-15% of body weight
  - Lateral ankle stability
  - Muscle attachment surface

### Ankle Joint (Talocrural Joint)
- **Type**: Hinge joint
- **Range of Motion**:
  - Dorsiflexion: 0-20°
  - Plantarflexion: 0-50°

### Foot Bones (26 per foot)
- **Tarsals** (7): Calcaneus, talus, navicular, cuboid, 3 cuneiforms
- **Metatarsals** (5): Numbered 1-5 medial to lateral
- **Phalanges** (14): 3 per toe except hallux (2)

## 2. Muscle Groups

### Anterior Thigh (Quadriceps Group)

#### Rectus Femoris
- **Origin**: Anterior inferior iliac spine
- **Insertion**: Tibial tuberosity via patellar tendon
- **Action**: Hip flexion, knee extension
- **Unique**: Only quad that crosses hip joint

#### Vastus Lateralis
- **Origin**: Greater trochanter, linea aspera
- **Insertion**: Lateral patella
- **Action**: Knee extension
- **Size**: Largest of quadriceps

#### Vastus Medialis
- **Origin**: Linea aspera
- **Insertion**: Medial patella
- **Action**: Knee extension, patella stabilization
- **VMO**: Oblique fibers crucial for patella tracking

#### Vastus Intermedius
- **Origin**: Anterior femur shaft
- **Insertion**: Base of patella
- **Action**: Knee extension
- **Position**: Deep to rectus femoris

### Posterior Thigh (Hamstring Group)

#### Biceps Femoris
- **Long Head Origin**: Ischial tuberosity
- **Short Head Origin**: Linea aspera
- **Insertion**: Fibular head
- **Action**: Knee flexion, hip extension, lateral rotation

#### Semitendinosus
- **Origin**: Ischial tuberosity
- **Insertion**: Medial tibia (pes anserinus)
- **Action**: Knee flexion, hip extension, medial rotation

#### Semimembranosus
- **Origin**: Ischial tuberosity
- **Insertion**: Posterior medial tibia
- **Action**: Knee flexion, hip extension, medial rotation

### Medial Thigh (Adductor Group)
- **Adductor Magnus**: Largest adductor
- **Adductor Longus**: Most superficial
- **Adductor Brevis**: Deep to longus
- **Gracilis**: Only one crossing knee
- **Pectineus**: Most superior

### Lateral Hip
- **Gluteus Medius**: Hip abduction, pelvic stability
- **Gluteus Minimus**: Hip abduction, medial rotation
- **Tensor Fasciae Latae**: IT band tension

### Calf Muscles

#### Gastrocnemius
- **Medial Head**: Larger, extends more distally
- **Lateral Head**: Smaller, higher insertion
- **Action**: Plantarflexion, knee flexion
- **Visible**: Creates calf contour

#### Soleus
- **Position**: Deep to gastrocnemius
- **Action**: Plantarflexion (primary)
- **Function**: Postural, slow-twitch dominant

#### Tibialis Anterior
- **Location**: Anterior compartment
- **Action**: Dorsiflexion, inversion
- **Visible**: Lateral to shin

### Deep Muscles
- **Popliteus**: Unlocks knee from extension
- **Plantaris**: Vestigial, variable presence
- **Flexor/Extensor groups**: Toe control

## 4. Motion Mechanics

### Gait Cycle Phases
1. **Stance Phase** (60%):
   - Initial contact
   - Loading response
   - Midstance
   - Terminal stance
   - Pre-swing

2. **Swing Phase** (40%):
   - Initial swing
   - Mid-swing
   - Terminal swing

### Key Muscle Activations During Walking
- **Initial Contact**: Tibialis anterior (dorsiflexion)
- **Loading**: Quadriceps (shock absorption)
- **Push-off**: Gastrocnemius/Soleus (propulsion)
- **Swing**: Hip flexors (leg advancement)

### Joint Angles During Normal Gait
- **Hip**: 30° flexion to 10° extension
- **Knee**: 0-60° flexion
- **Ankle**: 10° dorsiflexion to 20° plantarflexion

## 5. Common Variations and Asymmetries

### Structural Variations
- **Q-angle**: 10-15° males, 15-20° females
- **Tibial torsion**: 10-15° external rotation
- **Femoral anteversion**: 8-15° normal range
- **Leg length discrepancy**: <2cm common

### Muscle Development Patterns
- **Sport-specific hypertrophy**
- **Dominant leg differences** (5-10% larger)
- **Age-related changes**
- **Gender differences in muscle distribution**

## 9. 3D Modeling Recommendations

### Edge Loop Placement
- **3 loops minimum** at knee joint
- **2 loops** at ankle joint
- **Radial loops** around muscle bellies
- **Spiral topology** following muscle fibers

### Deformation Considerations
- **Volume preservation** during flexion
- **Skin sliding** over bone prominences
- **Muscle bulge** compensation
- **Fat pad** compression zones

### Level of Detail Guidelines
- **Hero**: All individual muscles defined
- **Medium**: Major muscle groups only
- **Low**: Basic form with normal maps
